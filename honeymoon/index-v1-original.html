üöó The Drive: Jacksonville to Islamorada

Total Distance: ~420 miles
Drive Time: 6-7 hours (without stops)
Best Route: I-95 South ‚Üí Overseas Highway (US Route 1)

üó∫Ô∏è Route Details

  1. Jacksonville to Miami (344 miles, ~4.5-5 hours)
    • Take I-95 South (most direct) or Florida's Turnpike (faster, has tolls)
    • Pass through: St. Augustine, Daytona Beach, West Palm Beach, Fort Lauderdale
  2. Miami to Islamorada (76 miles, ~1.5 hours)
    • Continue south through Homestead/Florida City
    • Enter the Overseas Highway (US Route 1) - one of America's most scenic drives!
    • Cross 42 bridges over turquoise waters
    • Islamorada is at Mile Marker 90-63

‚≠ê Pro Tips for the Drive

üåÖ Best Time to Drive

  • Start early morning (6-7 AM from Jacksonville) to avoid Miami traffic
  • Avoid Friday afternoons and Sunday evenings (peak traffic)
  • Weekday drives are generally smoother
  • Sunrise drive through Miami can be spectacular!

üåä Scenic Route Upgrade: Card Sound Road

Once you reach Homestead/Florida City, take Card Sound Road instead of continuing on US-1. Adds only 15 minutes but offers:

  • Less traffic
  • Beautiful water views
  • Alabama Jack's - legendary waterside bar and restaurant!
  • Access to Dagny Johnson State Park (beautiful tropical trails)
‚õΩ Fuel Up! Fill up in Jacksonville and again before entering the Keys. Gas prices increase as you go south. Last affordable gas: Homestead/Florida City area.
üìç Mile Markers: In the Keys, addresses use Mile Markers (MM). They count DOWN from 127 (Florida City) to 0 (Key West). Amara Cay Resort is around MM 80. This is how locals give directions!

‚úÖ REEF-SAFE SUNSCREEN (CRITICAL!)

Regular sunscreen kills coral reefs

  • Look for: zinc oxide or titanium dioxide based
  • NO oxybenzone or octinoxate
  • Brands: Thinksport, Sun Bum Mineral, Kokua, Supergoop Unseen
  • Hard to find in Keys - BRING FROM HOME!
  • Apply 15 minutes before sun exposure

‚òÄÔ∏è Sun Protection

  • Wide-brim hat (for her)
  • Baseball cap (for him)
  • Sunglasses (polarized for water)
  • Lightweight long-sleeve swim shirts (UPF protection)
  • Lip balm with SPF
